I'm Sizhe Wei, a master's student in Information and Communication Engineering, supervised by Prof. Siheng Chen and Prof. Ya Zhang at Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU).

Currently, I'm a visiting student at New York University (NYU), advised by Prof. Chen Feng.

I obtained my B.Eng in Information Engineering at Shanghai Jiao Tong University with the honor of being an outstanding graduate in 2021. I visited National University of Singapore (NUS) in the summer of 2019, working on the explainable AI supervised by Prof. Bingsheng He and Prof. Zeyi Wen.

My research focuses on multi-agent collaborative perception, improving teamwork among autonomous vehicles and robotics. My project experience includes object detection, tracking, and multi-agent prediction. I also possess expertise in graph signal processing and graph neural networks.

MAUC Explainable AI System: a Model-Agnostic and User-Centic Approach

Jun 2019 - Sep 2019 | Advisor: Prof. Bingsheng He & Prof. Zeyi Wen

  • Address limitations in existing Counterfactual Explanation methods on comprehensiveness, typicality, and computation efficiency.
  • Introduce iCE, which incorporates weighted k-means clustering into the CE process to generate comprehensive explanations with multiple counterfactual instances and global insights.
  • Experiments on six datasets show that iCE outperforms existing methods in terms of comprehensiveness, typicality, and computation efficiency.
